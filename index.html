The difference

We teach correct technique from day one.

  • Correct body position
  • Correct head position
  • Correct engagement with the water

We don't want your kids flapping around the pool with their head and body position all over the place, as we see so often. We want them to swim efficiently, become safe, and have fun.

Common questions

Things parents ask us

Why don't you have a level system?

Because strict levels stifle progress. Kids often get stuck in a level over one small skill deficiency, which blocks progress on everything else, and that breeds frustration, boredom and disengagement.

Instead, your child stays with one instructor who works with similar-ability kids, extends them on their strengths and helps with what they struggle with. Constant progression, no waiting rooms. Read the full explanation.

Why only four kids per class?

A 30-minute class isn't long, so we have to get the most out of it. Four is the most we think one instructor can properly coach. Any more than that is essentially crowd control. And we don't just say we have small classes: we guarantee it.

Will my child be pushed too hard?

No. We're not looking to train Olympians. Our programs were developed by our Head Coach with a psychologist, and they run on a process of improvement, not shouting, pressure, or a myopic focus on results. In our experience that leads to more enjoyment, less burnout and, ironically, better results. More on process over pressure.

What age should my child start?

Most of our swimmers start at age 4 or 5, and most of those are swimming 1–2km with efficient technique by age 8 or 9. Teaching correct technique is much easier when children first start swimming than fixing bad habits later. Older starters are very welcome too.

Which program is right for my child?

New and developing swimmers start in Learn to Swim; Pre-Squad builds fitness and endurance on top of technique; Squad is for competitive and fitness swimmers. If you're not sure, pick "Not sure yet" when you book a free lesson and we'll suggest a class when we call.
